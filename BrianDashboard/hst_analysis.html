CRITICAL CONTEXT: Division Contraction

2023–2025: Deako operated across ~125 DRH divisions with approximately 86,000 eligible homes. Oct–Dec 2025: ~10 divisions departed early. January 1, 2026: The footprint contracted to 27 divisions (~22,000 homes) — a 78% reduction.

All volume comparisons involving 2026 data must account for this. Raw YoY declines in pack counts, revenue, and HST/ITM totals are expected and do not indicate program failure. The meaningful metrics post-contraction are conversion rates (packs per close/eligible home) and per-division output — not absolute volume.

Analysis: How HST Direct Has Impacted ITM Pack Sales & Conversion Rate — And Is a Combined Effort Best for Deako?

Finding 1 — ITM Volume Dropped When HST Launched and Has Not Recovered

In 2023, ITM averaged 106 packs/month as the sole channel. When HST Direct launched in January 2024, ITM dropped to 73/month in 2024 and 69/month in 2025 — a sustained 35% decline. Whether HST caused this or broader market forces did, the reality is that ITM alone cannot carry the volume it once did.

Finding 2 — HST Direct Has Become the Dominant Channel (~2:1)

In 2024, HST averaged 122 packs/month vs 73 for ITM. In 2025: 134 vs 69. Combined, the two channels deliver 200+ packs/month, nearly double what ITM delivered alone. HST now accounts for 60–77% of all packs in any given month. This is not a failure — it is a second engine that is pulling significant weight.

Finding 3 — Combined ITM + HST Is Growing the Total Market

In 2023, total DPUP packs were roughly 1,272 (ITM only). In 2024, ITM+HST combined reached 2,337 packs. In 2025, 2,429 packs. The total addressable market for Deako is larger with both channels operating together. HST is reaching buyers who likely would never have converted through an in-person ITM event — that is genuinely additive volume. A combined HST + ITM strategy is clearly best for Deako's total pack volume.

Finding 4 — ITM Conversion Rate Has Declined Significantly

The ITM conversion rate averaged 3.2% in 2023, fell to 2.1% in 2024, and dropped further to 1.5% in 2025 — a 53% decline in two years. Some of this may reflect HST capturing the easiest conversions upstream, leaving ITM trainers with harder-to-convert buyers. But it also means ITM needs more support, better tools, or sharper targeting to regain effectiveness — not elimination.

Finding 5 — The Channels Serve Different Roles

ITM is a relationship-driven, high-touch channel — reps sell in the moment during a training interaction. HST is a passive, always-on channel — buyers purchase directly without a training event. These are complementary, not competing. The strongest divisions likely have both: HST capturing volume at scale, and ITM driving deeper engagement, certification, and brand loyalty among sales agents.

Finding 6 — Seasonal Patterns Affect Both Channels Differently

HST peaks in Q2 (spring selling season) and mid-year. ITM dips in Q2 historically. Some apparent inverse correlation may simply reflect seasonal forces. Q3 2025 (Jul–Sep) shows HST=122 avg and ITM=46 avg — the widest quarterly gap. Accounting for seasonality, both channels have their strong quarters, reinforcing the case for running both year-round.

Finding 7 — The January 2026 Contraction Changes Everything

On January 1, 2026, Deako's DRH footprint went from ~125 divisions (~86K homes) to 27 divisions (~22K homes). This is a 78% reduction in addressable market. Every volume metric — ITM packs, HST packs, revenue, profit — will show dramatic YoY declines that have nothing to do with program effectiveness.

The 2026 data (Jan–Feb showing ITM avg 29/mo, HST avg 118/mo) must be read through this lens. With only 27 divisions, even 50 ITM packs/month represents stronger per-division output than the 73/month across 125 divisions in 2024. The Q4 2025 decline (Oct–Dec) also partially reflects the ~10 early departures, not organic weakness. Conversion rate is now the only apples-to-apples comparison metric across years.

Verdict: Is a Combined HST + ITM Strategy Best for Deako?

Yes — unambiguously. From 2023 to 2025, total pack volume nearly doubled after HST launched. While ITM volume declined from 106/mo to 69/mo, the combined ITM+HST output (200+/mo) far exceeded what ITM achieved alone. Eliminating either channel would shrink the total market. The right question is not "which channel wins?" but "how do we optimize both within the new 27-division footprint?"

ITM needs investment, not abandonment. The conversion rate decline (3.2% → 1.5%) signals that ITM trainers may need better targeting (focus on communities with low HST penetration), refreshed training materials, or more frequent follow-up visits. ITM is still the channel that builds relationships with sales agents, creates certified advocates, and establishes model home experiences — none of which HST does. With only 27 divisions remaining, the training team can go deeper instead of wider — this is an opportunity, not a setback.

HST is the volume engine. It requires less hands-on management, scales across all divisions simultaneously, and captures buyers who would never attend an ITM event. Let it run.

The contraction resets the baseline. Comparing 2026 absolute volumes to 2023–2025 is meaningless — the addressable market shrank 78%. The metrics that matter going forward are: conversion rate per eligible home, packs per division per month, and combined ITM+HST output per division. If the 27 remaining divisions can maintain or improve their per-division rates, the program is healthy regardless of what the raw totals show.

How to Tell the Builder Story: Their Division IS Seeing Value

Critical context: HST Direct pack sales are not shared with the builder — only ITM pack sales are visible to them. This creates a perception gap: a builder may look at their ITM numbers, see modest or declining sales, and conclude that Deako isn't gaining traction in their division. That conclusion would be wrong.

When a division shows low ITM numbers but has active HST Direct sales, the reality is that their buyers are choosing Deako — just through a different channel. The demand exists. The product is selling. The builder's communities are generating pack sales. They just can't see 60–77% of them.

The message to the builder: "Your division is actually performing. Combined pack sales (which include sales we can't share individually but can reference in aggregate) show strong buyer adoption in your communities. The ITM number you see is the floor, not the ceiling. Deako is adding value across your homes — your buyers are voting with their wallets."

Post-contraction context (2026): Your division is one of 27 that Deako chose to stay with — down from 125. That means more focused training resources, deeper support, and a stronger partnership. The divisions that remain are the ones where Deako sees the most long-term value. Frame this as a commitment, not a retreat.

Talking Points for Builder Conversations
  • Lead with buyer demand data: "Across all DRH divisions, 1 in 3 homebuyers purchase simple Deako upgrades and 1 in 8 purchase smart upgrades — on their own, after closing. The demand for this product is built into your buyer base."
  • Show the money left on the table: "On average, $96 per home in Deako upgrade revenue is being captured after close by Deako directly — not by Horton. In some divisions, that number is over $200/home. ITM pack integration at time of sale lets Horton capture this revenue instead."
  • Reference aggregate volume without specifics: "Our data shows your division's communities are among the most active for Deako pack purchases across all sales channels."
  • Reframe ITM as one piece: "The ITM numbers you see represent our in-person training events. That's one of several ways buyers are choosing Deako in your homes."
  • Focus on the model home experience: "The strongest conversion driver is the model home setup — buyers who interact with Deako in the model convert at 2–3× the rate. Let's make sure every model is equipped."
  • Show the training investment: "We've trained X reps, certified Y agents, and set up Z model homes in your division — that infrastructure is what makes Deako sell itself."
  • Use the unrealized value hook: "Your buyers are already spending money on Deako after they close. Every dollar they spend post-close is a dollar your division could have earned through ITM. We can help you capture that."
