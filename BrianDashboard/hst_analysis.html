Analysis: How HST Direct Is Impacting ITM Pack Sales & Conversion Rate (Since Jan 2024)

Finding 1 — ITM Fell Immediately When HST Launched

In 2023, ITM averaged 106 packs/month with no competing channel. The month HST Direct launched (January 2024), ITM dropped and never came back — averaging 73/month in 2024 and 69/month in 2025. That is a sustained 35% decline relative to the pre-HST baseline. The timing is not coincidental.

Finding 2 — HST Direct Now Outsells ITM ~2:1

In 2024, HST averaged 122 packs/month vs 73 for ITM — a 68% premium. In 2025, that gap widened: 134 vs 69. Combined, these two channels are selling roughly 200+ packs/month, but the balance has shifted decisively toward HST, which now accounts for 60–77% of all packs in any given month.

Finding 3 — Inverse Correlation Is Real but Moderate

A scatter plot of HST vs ITM by month produces a negative trendline. Looking at month-over-month direction changes, the inverse relationship holds roughly 60–65% of the time: when HST rises, ITM tends to fall, and vice versa. It is not perfectly inverse — some months both rise — but the pattern is consistent enough to be meaningful.

Finding 4 — ITM Conversion Rate Has Collapsed

The ITM conversion rate averaged 3.2% in 2023, fell to 2.1% in 2024, and dropped further to 1.5% in 2025 — a 53% decline in two years. This cannot be explained by fewer eligible homes alone. HST Direct appears to be capturing buyers who would otherwise have been strong ITM candidates, leaving the harder conversions for ITM trainers.

Finding 5 — HST Is Expanding Total Volume, Not Just Replacing ITM

In 2023, total DPUP packs were roughly 1,272 (ITM only). In 2024, ITM+HST combined reached 2,337 packs. In 2025, 2,429 packs. The total market is larger — HST is not simply a 1-for-1 replacement of ITM. It is reaching buyers who likely never would have converted through an in-person ITM training event.

Finding 6 — Seasonal Patterns Complicate the Picture

Both channels show seasonal patterns. HST peaks in Q2 (spring selling season) and mid-year. ITM also dips in Q2 historically. Some of the apparent inverse correlation may simply reflect the same seasonal forces affecting both channels differently. Q3 2025 (Jul–Sep) shows HST=122 avg and ITM=46 avg — the widest quarterly gap on record.

Verdict: The Bottom Line Since January 2024

HST Direct is partially cannibalizing ITM, and the data makes a strong case. The 35% sustained drop in ITM pack volume that began the exact month HST launched — and has held for two full years — is not a coincidence. When a high-volume sales channel targets the same buyer (new home owners in the same communities), resources get split, attention gets divided, and the easier conversions get captured upstream by HST, leaving ITM trainers with a harder, more reluctant audience. That is why the conversion rate has fallen more than the raw pack count — the quality of the ITM opportunity is declining, not just the quantity.

However, HST is also genuinely growing the pie. Total packs sold (ITM + HST combined) nearly doubled from 2023 to 2024 and grew again in 2025. The program is reaching buyers who would not have attended an ITM event. That is real, additive value — and it shows HST is not simply a rebranded ITM.

The concern worth watching: If the ITM conversion rate continues its current trajectory — 3.2% (2023) → 2.1% (2024) → 1.5% (2025) → 0.75% pace in early 2026 — ITM risks becoming a marginal channel relative to HST. The question for your team is whether ITM investment is being right-sized given that HST now delivers roughly 2× the monthly volume. If ITM resources are being stretched across both programs, that would explain both the volume drop and the conversion rate collapse — and would point to a staffing or prioritization solution rather than a market problem.
